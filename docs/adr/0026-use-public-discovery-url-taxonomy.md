# Use Public Discovery URL Taxonomy

TripOS will use separate public URL families for the product marketing site, organizer discovery, trip pages, and demand pages. The root path is the **TripOS Marketing Site**. Organizer pages use `/organizers/{organizerSlug}/`. Public trip pages use `/organizers/{organizerSlug}/trips/{tripSlug}/`. SEO demand pages use `/trips/{demandSlug}/`, such as `/trips/darjeeling-from-bihar/`. The `/trips/` route can later become the **Public Discovery Catalog** index.

This keeps discovery URLs stable while preserving domain ownership. **Organizer Public Pages** and **Demand Pages** distribute demand to published trips, and Public Discovery may own discovery routing around published **Public Trip Pages**. **Public Booking**, booking URLs, checkout, payments, and trip operations remain owned by the organizer/trip operating domain. The route shape avoids treating a demand page as a specific trip and avoids turning TripOS into a true marketplace with platform-owned checkout or split settlement.

The main alternative was keeping public trip pages at `/trips/{organizerSlug}/{tripSlug}/`, which matches the current MVP route shape but creates ambiguity once `/trips/{demandSlug}/` demand pages exist. Another alternative was putting demand pages under `/destinations/`, but the immediate SEO intent is broader than destinations because pages can encode origin-to-destination demand such as "Darjeeling from Bihar". The chosen taxonomy reserves `/trips/` for discovery demand and gives concrete organizer-owned trips a clear organizer namespace.
