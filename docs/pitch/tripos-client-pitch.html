The client pain

Group-trip teams sell through Instagram, WhatsApp, and communities, then operate the trip across payment links, screenshots, spreadsheets, forms, and chat follow-ups. The result is not just admin work. It is operational risk before departure.

Payment confusion

UPI screenshots, partial transfers, late payments, and unclear balances sit across chat, bank records, and spreadsheets.

Capacity uncertainty

Seats appear available until someone reconciles who has actually paid the Reservation Amount.

Readiness chasing

IDs, emergency contacts, travel logistics, and medical disclosures arrive late or get buried in threads.

Repeated coordination

Pickup points, packing lists, reminders, and changes are manually resent across WhatsApp groups.

Positioning

TripOS is not a travel marketplace, hotel engine, flight product, or WhatsApp clone. It is the system of record for paid group-trip operations: bookings, money, readiness, reminders, and exports.

The workflow clients understand immediately

1. Create

Create a paid Trip with packages, capacity, dates, and Reservation Amount.

2. Open

Open booking only when payment setup is ready, while still supporting approved manual payments.

3. Reserve

Reserve all travelers in a booking together only after the full reservation amount is collected.

4. Confirm

Confirm bookings manually once documents, logistics, and other requirements are satisfied.

Why this matters

The organizer sees active reserved travelers, unpaid balances, overdue money, missing requirements, manual payment approvals, and operational exports without rebuilding the spreadsheet every day.
